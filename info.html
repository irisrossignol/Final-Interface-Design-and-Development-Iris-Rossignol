INFORMATIONS

PROMPT AND TECHNIQUE :

My first idea was to use the prompt "Skeuomorphism" because it was a prompt that inspired me. I decided to make a known game concept, I hesitated between Tic Tac Toe, Connect Four or chess and I chose Tic Tac Toe because it was the game that seemed the most accessible for me. I had trouble deciding on an aesthetic for my project and while trying different typographies I came across Chalkduster, a typo that looks like chalk and I had the idea of ​​making my Tic Tac Toe with the aesthetic of a chalk board with a slightly childish vision.

TECHNICAL APPROACH TO IMPLEMENTATION :

I really wanted to add a math.random in my code because it's the first code that struck me and I really like randomization. For the website implementation I put the player interaction, each player can either an X or an O and the first one who makes a line of three X or three O wins. Each time a player places his shape, the grid makes a sound, each cell makes a different sound. I had previously put my math.random on the letters X and O but after feedbacks I decided to change my math.random and put it on the colors of the texts on the right and left of my site. To replace the color of the X and O I chose to give the option to the players to choose between 5 colors of chalk for their shape. And finally we have the Restart button, which resets the game to 0, which changes the colors of the texts on the right and left and which resets the original chalk colors (red for the X, blue for the O).

PROMPTS AND FEEDBACKS

My two prompts were:
1. Are the colors of the letters too distracting?
2. Is the interface interesting and visually appealing, or does it feel too empty?
For the first, the answers were kind of mixed: half yes, half no. A few people said that the fact that all the Xs and Os weren't the same color was quite distracting (although others said it wasn't distracting since they were focused on the game). I wanted to add a math.random on these letters and differentiate them by putting different colors for each (the Xs are yellow/orange/red and the Os blue/purple/green). I thought that would be enough to distinguish the Xs and Os, but apparently not for everyone. To solve this problem I decided to move the math random to the right and left sentences of my game and to let the players choose their color for the game. For the second prompt, some people were saying that the site was a little empty in terms of interaction so I added the choice of chalks and some reviews also advised me to make the game bigger which was done.
